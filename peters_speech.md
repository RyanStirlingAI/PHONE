# Winston Peters -- Speech on the Electricity Industry

Ladies and gentlemen.

I want to talk to you tonight about something every single one of you already knows. Something you're reminded of every time your power bill arrives. Something the politicians in Wellington have been too comfortable, too compromised, or too cowardly to say out loud.

Your electricity system is broken. It was broken deliberately. And the people who broke it are laughing all the way to the bank while ordinary New Zealanders are sitting in the dark.

Now, I have been in politics a long time. Long enough to remember when New Zealand generated power for the benefit of New Zealanders. Long enough to remember when governments built dams and power stations because that was their job -- to serve the people. And long enough to have watched, year after year, as a small group of corporations captured our electricity market and turned it into a licence to print money.

They've been trying to get rid of me for forty years. I'm still here. And I'm still saying the things that need to be said, whether the comfortable classes like it or not.

Let me give you the facts. Because facts matter, even when certain people in Wellington would rather you didn't hear them. And the media won't tell you this, because they're too busy running opinion polls and chasing clicks.

The big four power companies -- Mercury, Meridian, Contact, Genesis -- control almost 90 percent of the electricity generation in this country. Ninety percent. And then they sell it back to themselves through their own retail arms. They generate it, they trade it among themselves, they retail it back to you. They set the price coming and going.

What sort of country do we live in where four companies can control the entire supply of something every family, every business, every hospital and every school depends on? What sort of market is that? I'll tell you what sort of market it is. It's a racket.

And the numbers prove it.

In the six months to December 2025, the combined operating profit of those four companies was approximately 1.86 billion dollars. Let me say that again. One point eight six billion dollars. In six months. That is a 44 percent increase on the year before. Forty-four percent.

Meanwhile, your power bills went up 12 percent last year. They're forecast to go up again this year. The average household is paying hundreds of dollars more a year than they were even three years ago. Ordinary New Zealanders are sitting in cold houses, putting on another jersey instead of turning on the heater, and these companies are banking record profits. Year after year.

Do I think the power companies are profiteering? I don't just think it. I know it. And I've known it for a long, long time.

Remember the winter of 2024? Wholesale electricity prices exceeded 1,500 dollars per megawatt hour. Fifteen times what the Australians were paying at the same time. Fifteen times. We are a country with mountains and rivers and wind and geothermal energy coming out of the ground, and we were paying fifteen times what the Australians pay. That is not a market functioning. That is a market failing -- by design.

Nobody else will tell you this. The gentailers -- and what a cosy little word that is -- the gentailers exist for one purpose. Net profit after tax. That's all they care about. That's all they've ever cared about. And their profits depend on constantly shorting the market. They have no interest in building more generation. Energy security? Couldn't care less. Scarcity suits their business model. They might build a wind farm here or there, but they will never build enough to bring the price down. Why would they? The less power available, the higher the price, the bigger the dividend.

Anyone who thinks these companies are going to deliver energy security for New Zealand is dreaming. They never have. They never will. It is against their financial interest to do so.

Now, you'll recall the so-called reforms of 1998. We were told -- solemnly, repeatedly, by very serious people -- that breaking up the old system would bring us cheaper power and better service. That was the promise. That was the sales pitch.

And what happened? Since those reforms we have seen the highest rise in electricity prices in our history. Every year, higher. And every winter it gets worse. The reforms didn't plateau prices. They turbocharged them. We have a disaster on our hands, and the architects of that disaster are still walking around Wellington collecting consultancy fees and congratulating themselves.

Look, I have spent my career saying things that other politicians are too nervous to say. So let me say something about this LNG levy that has certain people tying themselves in linguistic knots. We should not be wasting our time here with different sort of language trying to explain things away. It's a tax. Of course, if you have to put your hand in your pocket, it's a tax. This one may bring a benefit at the same time. But that's what it is. Call a spade a spade. That is what plain common sense looks like. And that same plain speaking is what we need on electricity.

You know, Michael Joseph Savage once said that the measure of a government is how it treats those who can least afford to help themselves. Well, by that measure, successive governments have failed New Zealanders on electricity for nearly thirty years. Savage would not recognise what his party has become. They privatised it, they corporatised it, they stood back and watched as the price climbed and climbed, and they told you it was the market working. With respect -- that is unadulterated nonsense.

So what is NZ First going to do about it?

I'll tell you exactly what we're going to do.

We are going to break up the power companies. Full stop. They will no longer control both the power and the price. We are going to split them into generators and retailers. Separate them. Completely. You generate power, or you sell power. Not both. Not anymore.

Now some people will say you can't do that. Watch us. Because that is the only way you get genuine competition in this industry. Right now, if you're a small power company trying to offer people a better deal, you can barely get a foot in the door. The big four own the generation, they own the retail, and they squeeze out anyone who tries to compete. That ends under NZ First.

But that's only the start.

We are going to set up a new Crown entity dedicated to geothermal drilling. New Zealand sits on one of the great geothermal resources in the world and we are barely using it. I'm talking about baseload power that doesn't depend on whether it rains or whether the wind blows. And I'm talking about jobs -- real jobs in Taupo, in Kawerau, in Whakatane, in the regions where this government and every government before it has left people behind. A government that was serious about energy security would be drilling. We are serious. We will drill.

We will introduce long-term fixed-price contracts for new generation. That gives investors a reason to build, and it gives New Zealanders the power supply they need. We will set up a new Ministry of Energy, because something this important to our national interest deserves a dedicated ministry, not some sub-unit of some mega-department where nobody can find it and nobody is accountable.

Now, if you're a homeowner, listen to this. If you generate power at home -- solar panels on your roof -- you should be able to sell it back to the grid at the same price you pay for it. The same price. Not some discounted rate that the power company dictates. Not some formula cooked up in a boardroom. The same price. That is fair. That is common sense. And under NZ First, that is what will happen.

What does all of this mean? It means more power stations getting built. More competition to bring your bills down. And a system that actually works when winter hits. That is how we take back control of our electricity system. For all New Zealanders. Not for the shareholders and the board members. For you.

The two old parties had decades to fix this. Labour talked about it and did nothing. National told you the market would sort it out. The market sorted it out alright -- sorted it out very nicely for the gentailers and very badly for you.

NZ First is the only party that will tell the truth about this industry and actually do something about it. We have always been that party. When everyone else was too afraid of offending the corporate donors, NZ First said what needed to be said. And we did what needed to be done.

Ladies and gentlemen, this is about more than electricity bills. This is about what sort of country we want to be. Are we going to let four corporations hold you to ransom every single winter? Or are we a country that says: enough. The lights stay on. The heaters stay on. And the bills come down.

I know which country I want to live in.

Back your country. Back New Zealand First.

Thank you.
